Python Finance Project: Bank Loan Analysis
 
Project Overview:
This project focuses on building a data-driven dashboard to analyze bank lending activities, evaluate risk, and monitor portfolio health through automated financial reporting.

Problem Statement:
The bank lacked a centralized system to track real-time lending performance, identify the financial health of borrowers, and distinguish between "Good" and "Bad" loans. There was a critical need to visualize monthly trends, regional disparities, and the impact of borrower profiles (like home ownership and debt-to-income ratios) on loan repayment.

Tools & Technologies Used:
To complete this analysis, the following Python ecosystem was utilized:
Data Processing: Pandas and NumPy for cleaning datasets, calculating Month-to-Date (MTD) metrics, and performing complex aggregations.
Statistical Analysis: Advanced filtering to compute Average Interest Rates and Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratios.
Data Visualization: Matplotlib & Seaborn for Bar Charts (Regional/Purpose analysis).
Plotly for interactive Line/Area Charts (Monthly trends) and Donut Charts (Loan terms).
Squarify for Tree Maps to visualize Home Ownership impact.

Key Merits:
Risk Mitigation: Clearly identifies Bad Loan percentages and amounts to help the bank adjust lending criteria.
Operational Efficiency: Automates the calculation of essential KPIs like Total Funded Amount and Total Received Amount.
Strategic Insights: Provides a visual breakdown of loan purposes and regional activity, allowing for targeted marketing and resource allocation.
Financial Health Tracking: Monitors borrower sustainability through Average DTI metrics.

Conclusion:
The Python Finance Project successfully transforms raw lending data into actionable business intelligence. By integrating automated KPI tracking with deep-dive visual analytics, the bank can now proactively manage its loan portfolio, minimize defaults, and optimize interest rate strategies for long-term profitability.
